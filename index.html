Microblading is often confused with other brow tattooing techniques. The chart below shows how each one differs in method, result, and longevity so you can choose the right option for your brows.

How the Procedure Works

A microblading session in Dubai takes 1.5 to 2.5 hours. Here's what you can expect:

  1. Consultation & brow mapping. Your technician assesses your face shape, brow bone structure, and natural hair pattern. A pencil is used to map the ideal brow shape and get your approval before any pigment touches the skin.
  2. Numbing. A topical anaesthetic is applied for 15–20 minutes to ensure the procedure is comfortable. Most clients describe the sensation as light scratching.
  3. Microblading. The technician uses a sterile hand tool with tiny needles to deposit pigment into the upper dermis layer. Each hair stroke is applied one at a time. The process takes 60–90 minutes for both brows.
  4. Pigment application. Pigment is massaged into the strokes and left to absorb before the excess is wiped away.
  5. Aftercare briefing. You'll receive detailed aftercare instructions, including a diagram of how to clean and moisturise the brows over the next 10–14 days.
  6. Touch-up booking. A follow-up appointment is scheduled for 4–8 weeks later to fill in any areas where the pigment didn't retain evenly.

Healing Timeline & What to Expect

Understanding the healing process helps you get the best result. Here's a week-by-week breakdown:

  • Days 1–3: Fresh and dark Strokes appear significantly darker than the final result. The brows may feel slightly tender and look thicker than expected. Some minor redness around the treated area is normal.
  • Days 4–7: Scabbing and flaking Fine scabs form along the strokes. The brows may feel itchy — do NOT pick or scratch. Let the scabs fall off naturally. Keeping the brows slightly moisturised helps.
  • Days 8–14: Ghosting phase After the scabs fall off, the pigment appears much lighter — sometimes 50–70% lighter than the initial result. This is temporary. The colour will re-emerge as the skin heals.
  • Days 15–21: Colour returns The pigment settles into its true shade. Strokes become visible and defined again. The brows look natural and healed.
  • Weeks 4–8: Touch-up A follow-up appointment refreshes any strokes that faded during healing. After this, the final result is set for 12–24 months.

Aftercare Tips for Dubai's Climate

Dubai's humidity and heat require extra care during microblading healing. The first 10 days are critical:

  • Keep brows dry. No swimming, steam rooms, saunas, or heavy sweating for 10 days. In Dubai's climate, this means avoiding outdoor workouts during the hottest hours.
  • No sun exposure. UV rays can alter pigment colour and slow healing. Wear a wide hat outdoors during the first 2 weeks.
  • Sleep on your back. Avoid pressing your brows into the pillow overnight for the first 7 days.
  • No makeup on brows. Skip brow powders, pencils, and concealers for at least 10 days.
  • Moisturise lightly. Apply the aftercare balm provided by your technician with a clean cotton bud — not your fingers.

Risks & Questions to Ask

Microblading is safe when performed by a certified technician. These are the main considerations:

✓ Safe When Done Right

  • Patch test identifies allergies beforehand
  • Results are semi-permanent — fades over 1–3 years
  • No downtime after first 3–4 days
  • Professional studios use sterile, single-use tools

⚠ What to Watch For

  • Oily skin may cause strokes to blur over time
  • Scarring if natural brow area has existing scar tissue
  • Colour may heal cooler or warmer than expected
  • DIY or poorly trained technicians can cause permanent damage

Questions to ask before booking:

  • What brand of pigment do you use, and is it hypoallergenic?
  • How many microblading procedures have you completed?
  • Can I see before-and-after photos of clients with my skin type?
  • Is the touch-up included in the price, and when must it be completed?
  • What is your redo policy if I am unhappy with the healed result?

Frequently Asked Questions

Is microblading painful?

Most clients report mild discomfort — described as a light scratching sensation. A topical numbing cream is applied 15–20 minutes before the procedure, which keeps discomfort to a minimum. Sensitivity varies by individual, but microblading is generally considered comfortable with proper numbing.

How long does microblading last?

Microblading typically lasts 12 to 24 months with proper aftercare. The longevity depends on your skin type (oily skin tends to fade faster), sun exposure, and skincare products used. Annual touch-ups help maintain the results.

Can I wear makeup after microblading?

You should avoid makeup on the brow area for at least 10 days after the procedure. After the initial healing, you can apply makeup as usual, though it's best to avoid harsh cleansers and exfoliants directly on the brows.

Is microblading safe for oily skin?

Microblading works on oily skin, but the strokes may soften and blur more over time compared to normal or dry skin types. Clients with very oily skin are often better suited to nanoblading or powder brows, which hold better on oily skin. A qualified technician can assess your skin during a consultation.

How much does microblading cost in Dubai?

Microblading in Dubai typically costs between AED 1,200 and AED 2,500 for the initial session including the touch-up. Combo brows (microblading + shading) range from AED 1,500 to AED 3,000. Always confirm what's included in the price before booking.
